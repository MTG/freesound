Updates to our Terms of Use and Privacy Policy

Hello dear Freesound user!

We have recently updated our terms of service and privacy policy and we need you to give us your consent before you can continue using Freesound. The updated terms of service and privacy policy are intented to clarify your rights as users, the ways in which we process your data and, in general, to protect you and us against incorrect uses of the site. This update does not alter the Freesound philosophy in any way, it is just a necessary step that we need to make in order to better comply with regulations such as the GDPR.

Please, take some time to read our terms of use and the privacy policy. We prepared a brief summary of them which we include here:

  • Uploaded sounds are released under one of the CC licenses you choose. Other content (metadata, tags, comments, etc...) is provided as CC-Zero.
  • Your activity in Freesound is public and can be seen by all users (except your private messages).
  • You are responsible for your posted content.
  • We (Freesound) keep your data confidential and apply security measures to keep this confidentiality.

  • We can use anonymized data collected from Freesound (sounds, statistics about sounds, tags, comments, downloads...) for research purposes.
  • You agree to receive email notifications in accordance with your email preferences.

In addition to the update of terms of service and pricavy policy, we also want to take this opportunity to upgrade current Creative Commons licenses from 3.0 to 4.0. The main difference between these two is that Creative Commons licenses 4.0 are more user-friendly and more internationally robust, but the general terms are the same. More information can be found here. The option to upgrade licenses is only offered to users who have uploaded sounds with any of the affected licenses.
